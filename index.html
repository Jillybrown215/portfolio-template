ABOUT ME

​I am a highly motivated individual who strives every day to be better than the day before because I learned at an early age that learning is about progress and growth. I am a native Philadelphian, raised by his mother in North Philadelphia. I attended Community College of Philadelphia and graduated with a duel Associate’s degrees in Psychology and Business Administration in May 2016. I am currently enrolled at Temple University (Fox School of Business) where I am majoring in Finance & Accounting with a minor in Management Information Systems.

​​My volunteer work includes Strawberry Mansion Learning Center where I tutor and serve as a mentor to students from K-12, Habitat for Humanity, and is the founder of a fine artist development organization called 215 On the Rise. 215 On the Rise is a non-profit organization that provides local artists a platform to showcase their talent. I also serve as a digital service fellow for The Urban Technology Project, an organization under the AmeriCorps umbrella. I chose the Urban Technology Project because it allows me to acquire transferrable skills in technology that I can teach others. Transferring this knowledge can potentially put high school students on track for becoming the next generation of leaders in the tech industry.
